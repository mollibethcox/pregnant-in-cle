Cleveland Clinic
Visitor Restrictions

Inpatient Locations: For all hospital locations in Ohio, we are restricting all visitors. Exceptions will be made for Labor & Delivery patients (1 visitor). All other visitors will be asked to leave the hospital.

Outpatient Locations: For all family health centers, medical office buildings and physician offices in Ohio, we are restricting visitors following these guidelines:

  • At this time, no guests or visitors are permitted to accompany adult patients, unless special assistance is required.
  • Visitors who are sick, have a fever, or a confirmed case of COVID-19 should not visit or accompany a patient.

University Hospitals
Visitor Restrictions

For maternity patients: Visitors are limited to the same one adult visitor for the duration of the patient's stay and will be screened.

Upon arrival, all visitors must check in at the front desk and be screened for:

  • Exposure or contact with COVID-19 suspected or diagnosed individuals in the last 14 days.
  • Visitor's temperature must be less than 100.0 degrees F and visitor must be free of flu symptoms: no cough, runny nose, diarrhea or shortness of breath.
  • Travel history -- No travel internationally, to New York City or on a cruise ship in the past 14 days.
  • Visitors will not be allowed to enter the facility if they screen positive for COVID-19.

CenteringPregnancy Programs and Prenatal Care at UH:

In-person appointments for prenatal care are ongoing. Patients must have an appointment or call ahead, and will be screened prior to entry. For appointments, call 440-623-7056.

CenteringPregnancy education and teaching is being offered virtually. New patients can sign up by calling 216-675-6667.

Northeast Ohio Neighborhood Health Services (NEON)
CenteringPregnancy

CenteringPregnancy is scheduled to start virtual services by the end of April/early May 2020. Resources are mailed to OB and Centering patients. Currently group sessions are on “pause” and patients are having traditional OB office visits until virtual groups are started. There are currently no visitor restrictions with these visits. Patients must call in before coming to a health center, and extra precautions are being taken (all patients must have temperature taken and complete a screening form). To register or make appointment for OB care call: 216-231-7700.


Moms & Babies First Program

Moms & Babies First is currently accepting new enrollments, and ongoing education is being offered virtually via video visits, phone contact, and social media. Based on eligibility, we are currently providing Pack N Plays, emergency diapers, formula assistance, food pantry items and emergency kits (household, cleaning and baby products). Participants who are NEON patients can see their provider for prenatal, pediatric and primary care services. For appointments call 216-231-7700.

Due to COVID-19 Virus, there are no in-person homevisiting services offered at this time. Resources are available to be delivered to participants to reduce the exposure and spread of the coronavirus.

For additional questions or enrollment inquires call the referral line at 216-231-7700 ext. 1028.

MomsFirst
Virtual Services

New enrollments and ongoing “visits” are being offered virtually via video visits and cell phone contact. Curriculum is being mailed out and dropped off with needed items to meet participant’s needs. Potential participants (those who are less than 33 weeks pregnant and city of Cleveland residents) can self-refer through our website or call and leave a message on our referral line (216) 664-4194.

Please note: no in-person services are being offered at this time.

Additional Information: Based upon need participants in the program qualify to receive emergency diaper assistance and Pack-n-Plays if in need of a safe sleep environment for the baby upon delivery. Participants can remain active in the program until the child turns 18 months old. Perinatal Depression screening for pregnant and postpartum women are offered as are child developmental screenings. Services also include health education and referral, case management and fatherhood services for dads of the baby.
